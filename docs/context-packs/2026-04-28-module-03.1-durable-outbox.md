## Outcome

Module 03.1 closes the SIGTERM-mid-PreToolUse data-loss window that the M03 closeout flagged as the audit-trail integrity gap. Every audit write (run_events, runs, policy_decisions across both apps) now goes through the `pending_jobs` table via `scheduleDurableWrite`, with a per-app `OutboxWorker` draining the queue on a 1-second tick. Both bridge and mcp-server boot paths now instantiate a worker, kick it after each enqueue for low-latency drain, and `await worker.stop()` on SIGTERM so in-flight dispatches land before the process exits. The crash-safety harness `verify-outbox-crash-safety.ts` proves the AC end-to-end: graceful SIGTERM and SIGKILL both result in the policy_decisions row landing after restart. Doctor checks 21/22/23 surface queue depth, oldest-pending age, and dead-letter count with OQ3-spec thresholds; check 13 transitions from permanent-yellow placeholder to GREEN.

## Decisions made (5 OQs locked at sign-off 2026-04-27)

- **OQ1 — cloud-mode strategy:** same `pending_jobs` table on Postgres (NOT BullMQ-on-Redis).
  - **Rationale:** avoids forcing Redis as a team-mode hard dependency. Postgres FOR UPDATE SKIP LOCKED gives the same exactly-once-claim semantics. Schema parity between solo and team is preserved.
  - **Alternatives considered:** BullMQ-on-Redis (rejected — adds infra dependency for low-volume audit traffic).

- **OQ2 — drain ownership:** each service (bridge + mcp-server) owns its own OutboxWorker, lease-serialized.
  - **Rationale:** dedicated drain worker would couple availability of one daemon to the other. The atomic UPDATE-with-LIMIT-1 (SQLite) / FOR UPDATE SKIP LOCKED (Postgres) ensures exactly-once-claim across N concurrent workers. Locked at OQ2 sign-off with the explicit add: a regression test in S1 covers the lease-expiry edge race.
  - **Alternatives considered:** central drain in a third process (rejected — coupling), or only the bridge drains (rejected — would orphan mcp-server's audits when bridge is down).

- **OQ3 — retry curve + dead-letter:** 1s/5s/30s/5min/30min, 6 max attempts, single-table dead-letter (status='dead' on pending_jobs). Doctor escalates: 0=green, 1–10=yellow, >10 OR any >1h = red.
  - **Rationale:** fixed schedule beats exponential-jitter at the audit volume here; the curve maps directly to operator SLOs. The 1h age trigger ensures small accumulations that get ignored bubble up to RED.
  - **Alternatives considered:** exponential backoff with jitter (overkill at low volume); separate dead-letter table (rejected — pending_jobs already has the right schema).

- **OQ4 — lease timeout:** 30 seconds.
  - **Rationale:** aligns with the bridge's SIGTERM grace window so a worker killed mid-dispatch doesn't have its row reclaimed before graceful shutdown finishes.
  - **Alternatives considered:** 5s (too aggressive — risks duplicate dispatch under transient load), 5min (too slow — recovery time unacceptable).

- **OQ5 — scope of replacement:** only audit-write callsites get the durable enqueue. Other setImmediate calls (none found audit-relevant) untouched.
  - **Rationale:** keep S2 surgical. Inventory matched: 5 bridge + 2 mcp-server + 1 shim deletion = 7 audit-write replacements + 1 deletion. No surprises during implementation.
